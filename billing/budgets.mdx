---
title: "Budgets and cost control"
description: "Per-zombie dollar ceilings, tenant-wide spend caps, and how debits work."
---

usezombie enforces budgets at two levels: per-zombie ceilings declared in `TRIGGER.md`, and a tenant-wide balance that every stage debits against.

## Per-zombie ceilings (`daily_dollars` / `monthly_dollars`)

Every zombie declares its own dollar ceilings in `TRIGGER.md`:

```yaml
budget:
  daily_dollars: 5.0
  monthly_dollars: 29.0
```

- `daily_dollars` caps spend over a rolling 24-hour window.
- `monthly_dollars` caps spend over the calendar month.

When either ceiling is reached the zombie stops processing new events and a budget breach is recorded in the activity stream. This is the first line of defence — one bad prompt never becomes an infinite burn on a single zombie.

Per-event token and wall-clock caps are internal runtime controls. They are not user-configurable in `TRIGGER.md` today.

## Tenant wallet

Separately, your tenant has a single wallet (the $5 free credit at signup, topped up via Stripe). Every completed stage debits this wallet.

- The wallet is **tenant-scoped**, not workspace-scoped. All workspaces under your tenant share the same pool.
- When the wallet hits **zero**, stages across all workspaces stop until the tenant tops up or upgrades to Scale.
- The wallet balance is visible in the dashboard header and via `zombiectl billing show`.

Set a monthly spend cap on your tenant from the billing page at [app.usezombie.com/billing](https://app.usezombie.com/billing).

<Note>A `zombiectl billing budget set` CLI command is planned for a future release.</Note>

## Run cancellation

Cancel an in-flight stage at any time via CLI or API:

```bash
zombiectl kill <zombie_id>
```

Cancelled stages stop immediately and do not debit your tenant balance — see [Credit deduction timing](#credit-deduction-timing) below for the full rule.

## Credit deduction timing

On the Hobby plan, credit is deducted only **after a stage completes successfully**. Failed and cancelled stages do not reduce your tenant balance.

On the Scale plan, usage-based billing follows the same principle: you pay for completed work, not failed attempts.
